# Through Their Eyes - Judge & User Guide

## Welcome!

**Through Their Eyes** is an AI-powered empathy experience that helps people understand conflicts from another person's perspective—not through a clinical report, but through an emotional journey.

## How to Use the App

### Step 1: Enter a Story
Write a brief conflict story from your perspective. Include:
- Who is involved
- What happened
- What you're feeling

**Example:**
"My father wants me to become an engineer, but I want to start a business. He says I'm risking my future, and I feel like he doesn't understand what I want."

### Step 2: Click "ENTER THEIR WORLD"
The AI analyzes your story and generates insights.

### Step 3: Experience the Journey

#### Section 1: **Core Conflict**
The emotional DNA of the conflict in archetype form.
- Example: `SECURITY vs FREEDOM`
- This is the central tension beneath everything

#### Section 2: **Misunderstanding Layer**
Reveals where communication breaks down.
- **Left:** What they mean underneath
- **Right:** What you hear
- **Click cards** to expand and explore deeper

**Why it matters:** Most conflicts aren't about disagreement—they're about misinterpretation.

#### Section 3: **Beneath the Argument**
Maps surface positions to deep emotional needs.
- Example:
  - Dad wants: "Become an engineer" → needs: **Security**
  - You want: "Start a business" → needs: **Freedom**

**Why it matters:** People argue about what they want, but conflicts are really about what they need.

#### Section 4: **Perspectives**
Click the three planets to experience:
- **YOU** - How you see the situation
- **THEM** - How they might see it
- **OBSERVER** - A neutral view of both

**Why it matters:** Every perspective is real and valid.

#### Section 5: **If You Were Them** (The WOW Moment)
A vivid 150-word narration describing what it's like to be the other person.

**Example:**
"You wake up tomorrow as your father. The first thing you feel is the weight of your own father's expectations—safety, stability, providing for family. When your child says they want to start a business, you hear: 'I'm rejecting everything you've taught me.' You wish they knew you're not trying to limit them; you're terrified something will go wrong."

**Why it matters:** This is where empathy clicks. Not understanding intellectually, but *feeling* what they feel.

#### Section 6: **Future Echoes**
See how three different responses play out over time:
- **Choice A:** Respond defensively
- **Choice B:** Respond with curiosity
- **Choice C:** Respond with empathy

For each choice, see:
- 1 Week Later
- 1 Month Later
- 1 Year Later

**Click each card** to expand and explore the timeline.

**Why it matters:** Empathy isn't abstract—it has real consequences over time.

#### Section 7: **Emotion Field**
Expandable bubbles showing each person's emotions.
**Click any bubble** to see their fears, goals, and assumptions.

#### Section 8: **Emotional Universe**
An interactive constellation map of the entire emotional landscape.
- **Drag nodes** to explore
- **Hover** to see connections
- **Colors show types**: Purple=Fear, Cyan=Hope, Orange=Anger, Pink=Love, Green=Goal, Yellow=Assumption

**Why it matters:** This isn't a technical diagram—it's an emotional landscape you can walk through.

#### Section 9: **Developer Mode** (Optional)
Expand this accordion if you want to see the technical details:
- Raw JSON output
- Validated data structures
- Implementation details

Most users won't need this—it's for developers and curious minds.

## Key Features Explained

### 1. Misunderstanding Engine
**Problem:** You say X, they hear Y. Communication breaks down.
**Solution:** Explicitly showing the gap between intention and interpretation.
**Result:** Users see where communication fails and can address it.

### 2. Hidden Needs Engine
**Problem:** Conflicts are about positions, not needs.
**Solution:** Mapping surface wants to deep emotional needs.
**Result:** Users understand what really matters.

### 3. Core Conflict Compression
**Problem:** Conflicts are complex and hard to summarize.
**Solution:** Distilling to archetypal emotional oppositions.
**Result:** Users see the heart of the matter instantly.

### 4. Future Echoes
**Problem:** Users don't see consequences of their choices.
**Solution:** Generating three futures based on response style.
**Result:** Users can see how empathy compounds over time.

### 5. Emotional Universe
**Problem:** Emotional data is abstract.
**Solution:** Creating an interactive constellation map.
**Result:** Users explore another person's emotional world.

### 6. Judge Wow Moment ("If You Were Them")
**Problem:** Empathy is hard to feel.
**Solution:** A vivid narration from their perspective.
**Result:** The emotional breakthrough moment.

## Sample Interaction

### Input Story
"My partner wants to spend more time together, but I need quiet time after work. They think I'm pulling away, and I feel pressured."

### Expected Output Flow

1. **Core Conflict:** `CONNECTION vs AUTONOMY`

2. **Misunderstandings:**
   - Partner means: "I love you and miss you"
   - You hear: "I don't think you love me"
   - You mean: "I need space to recharge"
   - They hear: "You don't matter to me"

3. **Hidden Needs:**
   - Partner's need: Belonging
   - Your need: Autonomy

4. **Future Echoes:**
   - Defensive: Isolation increases, connection weakens
   - Curious: You understand each other better, find compromise
   - Empathic: You prioritize time together AND protect recharge time

5. **If You Were Them:**
   "You wake up as your partner. Your first feeling is loneliness. You interpret the quiet time as rejection. You wish they knew that your need for closeness isn't pressure—it's how you feel loved..."

6. **Emotional Universe:**
   Interactive map showing: loneliness, love, overwhelm, need-for-space, anxiety, resentment, hope

## FAQ for Judges

**Q: Is this therapy?**
A: No. It's perspective-taking technology. It doesn't treat, diagnose, or advise.

**Q: How do I know it's accurate?**
A: You don't—and that's intentional. The prompt explicitly avoids diagnostic language. It's exploratory, not prescriptive.

**Q: What if both people are wrong?**
A: The app doesn't judge right/wrong. It shows both perspectives as equally real from their viewpoints.

**Q: How does it handle harmful relationships?**
A: The app explores perspective without excusing harm. It's a tool for understanding, not for minimizing abuse.

**Q: Is the JSON output part of the experience?**
A: No. It's hidden in Developer Mode. The experience is visual and emotional, not technical.

**Q: Can this replace mediation?**
A: No. It's a tool for individual insight, not conflict resolution between parties.

**Q: Why the cosmic dark theme?**
A: It creates a contemplative space. Dark sky, glowing constellations, quiet exploration. Perfect for emotional work.
